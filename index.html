Cristano Ronaldo Dos Santo Aveiro:
Ronaldo, 40, told CNN on Tuesday the 2026 World Cup, co-hosted by the United States, Canada, and Mexico, will “definitely” be his last. He also affirmed his plans to retire “soon” and detailed a possible timeline. “Let’s be honest, when I mean soon I mean probably one, two years,” Ronaldo told anchor Becky Anderson. “I’m enjoying the moment. But when I mean soon, it’s really soon, because I give everything for football. I’m in the game for the last 25 years, I did everything.” Once the Portuguese national team guarantees its World Cup berth—which could happen as early as this week—Ronaldo is set to become the first male player to ever appear in six different tournaments.
